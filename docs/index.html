What is the Millenium Fellowship?

The millenium fellowship is a semester long leadership development program that elevates leadership. Students come together to work on a project which helps to bring your campus community closer to one of the seventeen goals for global development

What is the Lions Electrical Discourse (LED)

We will offer green consulting solutions to Loyola Marymount University administration to make campus operations more sustainable, while also educating the student body. Our two main objectives are to promote sustainable lighting and power sourcing, which are both sustainable for the environment and cost efficient for the university.

First, L.E.D. lighting is about 8% cheaper than the fluorescent lighting that is currently common across campus. Next, power strips and outlets with on/off switches stop the unnecessary flows of energy when such power points are not in use. Additionally, motion sensor lighting can regulate when lights are powered on, as opposed to current 24 hour activation. This prevents costs of unnecessary energy consumption. Many universities worldwide already use such tactics, and it's time LMU joins them in doing right by the planet.

Our team brings together multiple disciplines from LMU’s Colleges of Business, Science & Engineering, Liberal Arts, and Film & Television to collaborate on our goals from scientific, fiscal, and social perspectives. We aspire to educate both our administrators and the student body on the efficiency of green alternatives in a multidisciplinary way. By implementing and advocating for sustainable energy solutions, we hope to position LMU for a greener future.

Future Goals

Loyola Marymount University has made a massive step towards carbon neutrality, aspiring to align its Westchester campus with the UNited Nations Sustainable Development Goal #11, Sustainable Cities and Communities. In addition to recent efforts to sustainably source more energy by means of solar panels and commitments to divest from fossil fuel industry holdings, the office of the President has committed to a plan to transition all indoor lighting from fluorescent to LED by the year 2025. Additionally, the office has announced that all available outlets on campus will be equipped with switches within the same timeframe.

While this change may sound small, the investment is expected to cut energy costs by millions in the years to follow. Further, by embracing this project, LMU is able to move its goal carbon neutrality date up from 2050 to 2038. Purchase of externally sourced electricity remains LMU's greatest direct contribution to Greenhouse Gas emissions, but with the help of this initiative backed by the UN Millennium Fellowship, LMU is well on its way to 0% external electricity purchase!

COVID Considerations

A remote situation is certainly possible with the project we have devised. First and foremost, our group members will be able to communicate remotely. All formal proposals can be developed virtually, and in turn, informational exchanges can be executed via video conferencing. Our project also has a growing amount of faculty support, and this network should allow us to facilitate positive and consistent communication with college deans and administrators.
